1. Reframe the tool’s role in your ecosystem

Current role

Standalone calculator.

High utility, low capture.

Target role

Top-of-funnel qualification tool that:

Educates.

Pre-qualifies.

Segments prospects by risk and readiness.

Routes them into the right Simplafi service.

Think of it as a “diagnostic entry point,” not a calculator.

2. Brand the experience without adding friction
A. Clarify ownership and authority

Right now, branding is only visible at the footer.

Improve with

Header sub-branding:

“Simplafi CMMC Readiness Tools”

Short credibility line under the title:

“Built by CMMC practitioners supporting DoD contractors nationwide.”

This establishes authority without sounding salesy.

B. Add contextual Simplafi touchpoints, not banners

Instead of ads, embed contextual guidance blocks:

Example placement

After SSP question:

“Organizations without an SSP cannot receive an SPRS score. This is the most common readiness blocker we see.”

Small “Why this matters” callouts reinforce expertise.

3. Turn results into a conversion moment
A. Score-based outcome tiers

After calculation, classify results:

Score Range	Label	Messaging Focus
90–110	Strong posture	Validation, maintenance
60–89	Moderate risk	Gap remediation
0–59	High risk	Contract eligibility risk
Below 0	Critical	Immediate action

Each tier should have one tailored next step, not multiple CTAs.

B. Soft CTA tied to risk, not sales

Examples

“Understand what controls are impacting your score”

“See how assessors validate this control”

“Map your score to a defensible SSP”

Only one CTA per tier.

4. Capture leads without forcing sign-ups
A. Progressive value exchange

Instead of “Sign in to save,” offer:

Save results as PDF.

Email summary with:

Score.

Top 5 deductions.

Common assessor pitfalls.

Email capture becomes natural, not mandatory.

B. Add assessor-language previews

Include small locked sections:

“How an assessor will interpret this answer”

“What evidence typically satisfies this control”

Unlock via email.

5. Use the tool to pre-qualify consulting engagements
A. Embed structured handoff data

When users opt in, capture:

Score.

SSP status.

Top failing control families.

Environment type (Commercial, GCC, GCC High).

This allows:

Faster discovery calls.

Higher close rates.

Better pricing alignment.

6. Position it as an industry resource, not a sales page
A. External marketing uses

LinkedIn posts:

“Before your next CMMC conversation, know your estimated SPRS score.”

Partner referrals:

MSPs, CPAs, primes can safely share it.

B. Credibility signal

“Used by DoD contractors preparing for Level 2 assessments.”

No logos needed. Language matters.

7. Monetization paths that preserve trust

You do not need to charge for the calculator.

Better options:

Free calculator.

Paid:

Readiness review.

Assessor-style control walkthrough.

SSP validation.

The calculator becomes proof of competence, not the product.
